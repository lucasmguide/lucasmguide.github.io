About

I am a human geographer specializing in the critical political economy of digital platforms, with a focus on the socio-spatial inequalities produced by platform logistics in the Global South. My work examines how algorithmic management reshapes labor relations and territorial dynamics, combining qualitative methods — including interviews and participant observation with platform workers — with spatial data analysis.

Drawing on urban theory and critical political economy, I investigate how platform companies reconfigure the circuits of the urban economy, generating new forms of precarity that cut across class, race, gender, and migration status. I contribute to debates on workers’ rights, epistemic justice, and the regulation of platform capitalism, with comparative research experience in Brazil and Europe.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Philosophy and Human Sciences (IFCH), University of Campinas (Unicamp), under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Ricardo Antunes. I completed my PhD in Geography in 2026 under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Ricardo Abid Castillo, and held a visiting researcher position at the University of Bologna (2024–2025), funded by a CAPES/PDSE scholarship.

I am a member of the Laboratory of Geographical Investigations and Territorial Planning (GEOPLAN–Unicamp), the Research Group on Logistics, Agriculture and Uses of Territory (LAUTER–Unicamp), and the Labour Tech Research Network. I serve as a peer reviewer for the journal Inequalities (Edizioni Ca’ Foscari, Ca’ Foscari University of Venice).

My research interests include platform economies, logistics, institutional markets, spatial production circuits, and the circuits of the urban economy.
